Marketing Campaign Analysis

A senior-level data science project focused on optimizing marketing outcomes through data engineering, customer segmentation, KPI analysis, and A/B testing.

🔍 Problem Statement

How can we increase campaign response rates and ROI by understanding customer behavior and tailoring campaign strategies across segments?

🧠 How I Think Like a Data Scientist

I don’t just analyze — I interrogate the data with a strategic mindset. This project was not about building a dashboard, it was about surfacing unseen signals that lead to real action.

  • Curiosity-led exploration: Instead of asking “Who responded?”, I asked “Who responded unexpectedly, and why?”
  • Business-first framing: I treated data as a decision-making tool, not a reporting mechanism.
  • Modeling for impact: I applied statistical testing not to prove a point, but to reduce uncertainty and risk in marketing decisions.

🚧 Problems I Faced & How I Tackled Them

  • Messy income data: ~1% missing values — resolved using targeted imputation after validating distributions
  • Ambiguous response variables: Merged multiple binary campaign flags into a consolidated Response metric to analyze performance holistically
  • Outlier bias: Boxplots showed extreme values in spending — I capped them to preserve general trends while minimizing distortion
  • Too many KPIs, not enough focus: Created clear metric categories: Acquisition, Engagement, Conversion — and mapped each SQL output accordingly
  • Statistical noise in A/B testing: Validated assumptions with t-tests and confidence intervals to prevent false-positive conclusions

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

  • Python (Pandas, Seaborn, Statsmodels)
  • SQL (KPI logic, segmentation queries)
  • Tableau for dashboarding and storytelling
  • A/B testing (control vs treatment, t-test)

⚙️ Project Flow

  • Raw data cleaning (null imputation, dtype checks, outlier treatment)
  • Feature engineering (RFM variables, total spend, recency)
  • Campaign KPI extraction using SQL
  • Segmentation with RFM, campaign response clustering
  • A/B testing to validate uplift and conversion gains

📊 Key Insights

  • Catalog campaigns had the highest response rate (7.4%)
  • Overall campaign conversion: 44.89%, with latest campaign at 15.05%
  • RFM segmentation showed stale customers responded better than expected
  • Top segments: Big Spenders and Champions
